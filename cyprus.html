Cyprus

Paphos Larnaca Nicosia
We landed in Paphos, which is the furthest place in Europe that Stansted flies to. We were picked up by graffiti artist/volunteer construction worker who turned out to be a living legend. We stayed in Larnaca with a wonderful family that treated us like family, a family of four that were full of energy and hospitality. I tried to learn a track by dave on their piano by th end of the holiday. We travelled to Nicosia which is right on the border of Turkey and Cyprus, they had some amazing food and at a very good price. We had halloumi pie, lahmacun, chicken kebab, pork kebab and beef kebab.

Famagusta

Famagusta used to be the biggest and well-built up part Cyprus until an invasion by Turkey claiming that it belonged to them. Because of this everyone was told to evacuate their homes with the view that they would come back when this issue was resolved. When I arrived almost fifty years later the entire beachfront it abandoned and is being looked over by the UN. It is crazy how many people are displaced from normal human living. It is a shame that individuals in this world ruin it for the rest of us by starting wars and causing conflict. It was such a tragedy to look at and it was a no-entry zone meaning you would be shot at the entrance to the site.
